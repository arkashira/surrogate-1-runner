## 1️⃣ Design Overview  

| Feature | Source | Unified Implementation |
|---------|--------|------------------------|
| **Input validation** | Both (custom `SimulationError` + FastAPI `HTTPException`) | Use a small hierarchy: `SimulationError` (sub‑class of `ValueError`) for internal use, and a thin wrapper `as_http_exception()` that converts it to a FastAPI `HTTPException` when the service is called from an API endpoint. |
| **Data schema** | Candidate 1: `base_demand` only  <br> Candidate 2: `base_price`, `demand`, `elasticity` | Support **both** minimal and richer schemas. The service detects which fields are present and computes revenue accordingly. This keeps the simple use‑case (only `base_demand`) while allowing the more expressive elasticity model. |
| **Price specification** | Candidate 1: explicit `price_points` list supplied by caller. <br> Candidate 2: internal multiplier grid (`_PRICE_MULTIPLIERS`). | The public `run()` method now accepts **either** a user‑supplied list of absolute prices **or** a list of multipliers. If the caller supplies `price_points`, they are used directly; otherwise the service falls back to the built‑in multiplier grid applied to each product’s `base_price`. |
| **Randomness** | Both seed‑based deterministic approach. | Keep a **per‑instance** `random.Random` object (`self._rng`) seeded in `__init__`. This avoids global state clashes when the service is instantiated multiple times (important for FastAPI’s dependency injection). |
| **Monte‑Carlo core** | Candidate 1: `price * base_demand * demand_factor` with `demand_factor ~ N(1,0.1)`. <br> Candidate 2: price elasticity formula. | Implement **two interchangeable revenue models**:  
  * **Simple model** – identical to Candidate 1 (good for quick tests).  
  * **Elasticity model** – `revenue = price * demand * (price / base_price) ** elasticity`.  
  The model is chosen automatically based on the presence of the `elasticity` field. |
| **Statistical output** | Both compute mean, std, 95 % CI, and an “optimal price range” (within 1 % of max). | Preserve that logic, but expose the **full distribution** per price (optional) and make the 1 % tolerance configurable. |
| **Performance guard** | Candidate 1: unit‑test asserts < 5 s for 1 000 iterations on 10 000 rows. | Add a tiny optimisation – pre‑compute the per‑row constant part (`base_price` or `base_demand`) once per iteration and use list‑comprehensions where possible. The service still meets the < 5 s bound on typical hardware. |
| **Testing** | Candidate 1 already supplies a solid test suite. | Keep those tests unchanged, add a few extra cases for the elasticity path, and expose a `__main__` demo. |
