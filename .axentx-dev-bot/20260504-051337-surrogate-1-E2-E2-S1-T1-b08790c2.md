# Discovery Stage Guide

## Overview
The **Discovery** stage is the first point of contact between a potential customer and your product. The goal is to generate awareness and attract the right audience.

## Key Objectives
1. **Identify target personas** – Define who benefits most from your solution.  
2. **Create high‑impact content** – Blog posts, social media snippets, and SEO‑optimized landing pages.  
3. **Leverage paid channels** – Run low‑budget ads on platforms where your personas spend time.

## Tactics & Examples

| Tactic | Description | Real‑World Example |
|--------|-------------|--------------------|
| **SEO Blog Series** | Publish a 3‑part series that solves a common pain point. Use keyword research to rank for long‑tail queries. | *“How to Automate Data Enrichment in 5 Steps”* – each post targets a specific keyword and links to the next. |
| **LinkedIn Thought Leadership** | Share short posts and carousel images that showcase industry insights. | Founder posts a weekly “Data Ops Tip” carousel that garners 200+ engagements per post. |
| **Micro‑Targeted Ads** | Run $10‑$20 daily campaigns on Reddit or Twitter aimed at niche communities. | A Reddit ad in r/MachineLearning drives 150 clicks to the landing page in one week. |

## Metrics to Track
- **Impressions** – Number of times your content is displayed.  
- **Click‑Through Rate (CTR)** – Ratio of clicks to impressions.  
- **Cost per Click (CPC)** – Spend divided by clicks.  

## Checklist
- [ ] Persona profiles completed and approved.  
- [ ] SEO keyword list generated (≥10 primary keywords).  
- [ ] At least 2 content pieces scheduled per week.  
- [ ] First paid ad campaign launched with defined budget.
