## 1. Overview
Divergence in a CFD simulation usually signals that the numerical solution is leaving the physically admissible space.  
Typical culprits include:

| Category | Typical symptom | Quick sanity check |
|----------|-----------------|--------------------|
| **Numerical settings** | Oscillations, blow‑ups, residuals that never drop | CFL < 1, under‑relaxation > 1 |
| **Mesh** | Localised spikes, non‑convergence | Skewness > 0.5, aspect ratio > 10 |
| **Boundary conditions** | Unphysical gradients, pressure spikes | Dirichlet vs Neumann mismatch |
| **Initial conditions** | Rapid divergence, no steady‑state | Start from a converged steady‑state or a simpler model |
| **Physical models** | Instabilities in turbulence or multiphase | Incompatible models, missing source terms |
| **Solver settings** | Residuals plateau, solver stalls | Convergence tolerance, pressure‑velocity coupling |

|------|--------|----------------|---------------|
| **1. | **2. Inspect the mesh** | • Compute skewness, aspect ratio, orthogonality.<br>• Look for hanging nodes or overlapping cells. | Poor mesh quality introduces numerical diffusion and local instabilities. | Refine the mesh locally where gradients are steep; use a mesh‑quality checker. |
| **3. | **4.
