## 1. Acceptance Criteria
- **AC1** – A user can register an account by providing a **valid email address** (RFC‑5322 compliant) and a **password** that meets the password policy (minimum 8 characters, at least one uppercase, one lowercase, one digit, one special character).  
- **AC2** – After successful registration, the system returns a **201 Created** response containing a **user ID** and a **JWT access token** (expires ≤ 1 hour).  
- **AC3** – A registered user can log in by submitting the same email and password and receives a **200 OK** response with a **new JWT** and a **refresh token**.  
- **AC4** – Invalid credentials (wrong email or password) result in a **401 Unauthorized** response with a generic error message (“Invalid credentials”).  
- **AC5** – Passwords are never stored or returned in plain text; they are hashed with **bcrypt (cost factor ≥ 12)** before persistence.  
- **AC6** – All login/registration endpoints are protected against **brute‑force** (rate‑limit: max 5 attempts per 10 min per IP) and **SQL injection** (prepared statements only).  
- **AC7** – Audit logs are created for each successful registration and login, capturing user ID, timestamp, IP address, and outcome (success/failure).
