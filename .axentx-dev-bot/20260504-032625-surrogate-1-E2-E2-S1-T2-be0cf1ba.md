## Unified Media‑Buffering Design
Below is a single, production‑ready implementation that pulls the strongest ideas from the two candidates:

* **Explicit, test‑driven configuration** (`config.py`) – keeps constants in one place and makes them easy to override in tests or production.
* **Async pre‑fetching with a thread‑pool** – keeps the event‑loop responsive while still using blocking I/O for disk reads.
* **Timeout handling** – logs a warning and falls back to an empty buffer if the read takes too long.
* **State machine & statistics** – gives you visibility into how often pre‑buffering hits/misses and how long buffering takes.
* **Clean shutdown** – shuts down the executor and cancels any pending tasks.
* **Comprehensive tests** – cover normal prefetch, lazy `get_buffer`, and timeout behaviour.

> **Contradiction resolution**  
> Candidate 1 used a simple `BufferManager` that only prefetched a fixed byte count.  
> Candidate 2 introduced a richer state machine and statistics.  
> The final design keeps the simple, efficient pre‑fetch logic of Candidate 1 but augments it with the state machine and stats of Candidate 2, while still honouring the timeout behaviour of Candidate 1.
